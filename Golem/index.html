In Hardware & Software Technologies, we had to build our own physical prototype that is linked to an application from scratch. The concept is based on a game that we should create. My group members were: Angelica Gomez, Andrea Simeone, and Chiara Santandrea. During our brainstorming phase, we created a simple game called Golem.Golem Garden is an integrated system based on the act of saving money, designed to help people organise their spare cents through an interactive and relaxing game. The system is integrated into 2 parts that are connected: A Golem-shaped money box prototyped with Arduino that counts money thanks to an infrared sensor. It gives light feedbacks and the money count is shown on an LCD screen. A smartphone game app where coins are transformed into energy that you can use to take care of a virtual garden. The corridor of the Golem was made to enclose the infrared sensor, which gives it more accuracy when registering the diameter of each coin. During our experiments, we discovered that in order to standardise the coin speed the corridor had to be slanted. This way we were able to assign a value to each coin depending on its diameter. Inside the body of the money box, we can find all the components of the circuit form the Arduino board, LCD screen, buttons, eye leds and the IR sensor with the related led. Arduino is linked with the phone via Bluetooth/cable connection giving the signal each time a coin is inserted, uploading the amount of coins both on the Lcd screen and in the app.A smartphone game app where coins are transformed into energy that you can use to take care of a virtual garden.
The app is composed of: 300+ trigger and responses 7 variables storing information, such as coin amount, energy amount, name, plant evolution parameters etc. 4 conditions managing the interaction between the energy and possible game actions.
