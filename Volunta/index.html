The second project we were assigned to in Digital Design Studio, was to create an application from scratch following a certain brief, which is mainly solving social issues by creating a citizen engagement platform. My group was composed of: Chiara Facchini, Laura Guerinoni, Michela Brambilla, and myself. We chose to create an application dedicated to matches volunteering offer and demand and connects potential volunteers who do not know how to contribute with NGOs recruitment processes. It’s called Voluntà.First, we conducted a desk research, in which we found that 65% of people use the digital without a strategic approach and 43% have low digital skills. In addition, they don’t have enough funds,, and need to change internal culture. There are other perceived challenges and need to reorganise processes/infrastructure.After the research, we created a benchmarking for volunteers and associations, which helped us identify what void we are trying to fill. As part of our ongoing research, we interviewed 4 small-medium sized neighbourhood associations in different areas of the Milan area. Also, we conducted a survey, in which we received 101 answers, 78 of which who did volunteer and 23 who didn’t. Based on all of those results, created an Ecosystem Map, as shown below:We developed two personas to support the development of the application, one for the associations that want to find new volunteers and one for the volunteer itself. They are both based on the interviews we conducted and on the data collected during the desk research. For the volunteer’s side, we decided to develop a User Journey that could help us to understand the main barriers to determine what limits the user in accessing the volunteering world. This representation is based on all the research we have conducted above.We developed 2 value propositions, one for the volunteers and another for the association. We also took into consideration the Public Administration, and determined their values.So we can determine the Information Architecture, we conducted a tree testing by giving the users 10 tasks to achieve and analyse the rate of success of each, taking into consideration their suggestions. The results is shown as such:After the tree testing, we structured the Information Architecture of both: the volunteers and associations; resulting to 2 different Information Architecture.The Information Architecture for the volunteers:The Information Architecture for the associations:If they’re signing up for the first time:If they’re signing up for the first time:After signing up:After signing up:Based on the Information Architecture, we began with the application design. The first step we took is structuring the application by creating a wireframe. From the wireframe, we designed a Lo-Fi application design. Before moving on the final design of the application, we conducted another test with the users. For the both sections, the users were composed of 10 young adults with an online tool and 5 of them were moderated tests where the users spoke their thoughts and concerns, using the tasks from the tree testing.Using the results, we fixed all that is needed to be fixed, and determined the design system in which we followed. Using the Mid-Fi prototype, we conducted the final design where it was a peer to peer review with our classmates, it was composed of 11 tasks: 6 for Volunteer part and 5 for Association part, in which we used a think out loud technique.

Finally, we have define our final concept: An app able to meet both the needs of the person who wants to volunteer and the association in a new way. It’s not the association that will display what it needs and wait to be found. The users will personalise their profiles to showcase what they can do / what they would like to work on, and the associations will be put in the conditions to easily contact the people they need.
