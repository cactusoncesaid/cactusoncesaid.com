In Final Design Studio, we had to create a project focusing on sustainability in a digital city. It was a group project, in which I worked with Angelica Gomez, Chiara Facchini, Laura Guerinoni, Silong Luo, Ying Wang, and Yuqing Su. Currently in sustainability, beneficial insects are not taken into consideration anymore, we are focusing on them in order to reduce the use of pesticide, pollinate crops, and increase biodiversity.Our first phase of our work progress began with a desk reasearch and benchmarking, and we set a question that will represent our work: “How might we design a service system for Citizens in smart cities, to change their perception towards beneficial insects and, involve them in helping promote their presence in the region?” After we conducted interviews with organisations and experts in the field.Our first phase of our work progress began with a desk reasearch and benchmarking, and we set a question that will represent our work: “How might we design a service system for Citizens in smart cities, to change their perception towards beneficial insects and, involve them in helping promote their presence in the region?” After we conducted interviews with organisations and experts in the field.The process of ideation we followed first began with the first concept we came up with then evolved to the final concept, which is Rebug, where citizens can adopt a house of ladybugs in a subscription modality from a local food producer and check on them from afar.There are 4 major touchpoints in Rebug, and it is represented as such:At the end of the game on the coffee vending machine, the users will see a QR code that will redirect them to the landing page. On there, they will see the score that they have achieved during the game, and a brief description about Rebug with a download button.At the end of the game on the coffee vending machine, the users will see a QR code that will redirect them to the landing page. Since it is composed of different types of data flows, we have classified them into sensor data flow, farmer data flow and other data flow. Finally, the AI is responsible for analysing the data and process them to feed them in the user app.To see all of the work: https://rebugproject.wixsite.com/rebugThe application of the app has these main features:First, you adopt a ladybug house, that will be shown after downloading the app. There is a step-by-step guidance as to how they can adopt a house.After adopting a house, users can stay updated and connected with everything that the users need to know about their living conditions. They can also “chat” with their ladybugs and receive pictures from the farmers that are taking care of them.There are other features in the application that includes: “My Profile” where they can see the progress of their gift box. Also, there is an educational section where the users can learn about the beneficial insects.First, the users will first encounter Rebug is through a game on the coffee vending machine while they wait for their coffee. The game is simple and fun, all the user have to do is move the ladybug along the screen and save all the coffee beans by eating all the pesticides. The goal is to fill the waiting time for their coffee to be ready and engage them to download the application.Second, we constructed a small prototype of the ladybug’s house that keeps track of the environment and the aphids count, to make sure everything is suitable of the ladybug’s habitat and progress.Last but not least, a fresh produce basket is a recurrent basked that is awarded to the users holding onto a membership for 3 months, it’s an earned reward that is a symbol of genuine gratitude for contributing from your sustainable countryside.Third, the application where the users can sponsor a new house, talk to our ladybug-bot and learn about insects.
