In Digital Art, we had to also work in groups (Angelica Gomez, Dorsa Rafiee, Jan Ostrowka, Miguel Barata, Rezeile Quirit, Silong Luo, Xiyuan Hu, and I) to create an exhibition inspired by Piet Mondrian. The theme that we have focused on is connecting Piet Mondrian’s neoplasticism with everyday life senses. Therefore, our concept is a multisensory experience that allows the viewer to become part of Mondrian’s art while following a tour through three interconnected rooms that bring his paintings to life. We divided the exhibition into 2 rooms and a connected hallway, to create a walkthrough of the experience we are trying to portray.As soon as the people walk in they will enter the first room, called the Nature Room. It’s composed of certain key elements, in which are: the tree, windmill and nature; portraying one of the key elements that is found in Mondrian’s earlier paintings. The experience that we are trying to deliver is focusing on the senses of touch, wind, and ambient sound.The room is structured as such:

Last but not least, the final room in the exhibition is called the City Room, describing the last phase of Mondrian’s work. The key elements that’s been used are the primary colours – the colours that Mondrian used in his paintings, light, and the concept of day and night. The users will experience ambient sound, mist, and jazz music.
